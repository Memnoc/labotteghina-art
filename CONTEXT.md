# La Botteghina

Portfolio website for an Italian digital painter blending modern digital technique (Wacom/Huion + Photoshop) with the old-master Italian school. The site's job is to present the work at gallery-grade perceived value.

## Language

**La Botteghina**:
The brand. Affectionate diminutive of *bottega* — the Renaissance master's workshop where apprentices trained and works were produced. The brand narrative is "old-master workshop, digital tools."
_Avoid_: "little office", "studio" (as a brand translation)

**Work**:
A single finished painting shown on the site. Carries a title, year, story, and medium/dimensions.
_Avoid_: Piece, artwork, project, post

**Story**:
The paragraph written in the artist's voice that accompanies a Work. Editorial content, not a caption.
_Avoid_: Description, caption, blurb

**Medium line**:
The Work's technical statement, e.g. "Digital, Photoshop, 4000×6000 px". Required if prints are ever sold.
_Avoid_: Specs, details

**Wall**:
The single curated, unfiltered gallery presenting all Works (10–25 at launch). Deliberately flat — no categories, filters, or pagination; curation over volume.
_Avoid_: Feed, grid, archive, gallery page

**Inquiry**:
A visitor's message about a specific Work, delivered by form to the artist's inbox. Not an order — no price, no checkout, no obligation.
_Avoid_: Order, purchase, request

**Hero**:
The small set of featured Works that open the home page in a slow crossfade. A Work is either on the Hero and the Wall, or only on the Wall.
_Avoid_: Banner, slider, carousel

## Flagged ambiguities

- "La Botteghina" was initially glossed by the artist as "the little office" — resolved to *little workshop* (bottega), which anchors the brand narrative.

## Example dialogue

> **Dev**: Should the Wall support filtering by category?
> **Artist**: No — the Wall is curated, not browsed. If a Work doesn't earn its place, it doesn't hang.
> **Dev**: And the text under each Work on its detail page — that's the caption?
> **Artist**: It's the Story. It's written in my voice, it's part of the work's presentation, not metadata. The Medium line is the metadata.
